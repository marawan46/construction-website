Concrete is the backbone of construction, yet it's also the source of some of the most expensive — and entirely preventable — mistakes in the industry.

## 1. Skipping the Slump Test

The slump test takes four minutes. Skipping it to save time on a poured slab can cost you the entire pour if the mix is off. We've seen it happen.

<Callout type="important">
  A concrete mix that's too wet loses up to **40% of its design strength**. That's not a small margin — that's a structural liability.
</Callout>

## 2. Rushing the Cure

Concrete gains most of its strength over 28 days, not 24 hours. Loading a slab too early is one of the leading causes of cracking that looks like a design failure but is actually a schedule failure.

## 3. Ignoring Ambient Temperature

Pouring in temperatures below 5°C or above 35°C without adjustments to admixtures or curing methods is a gamble. The cost of a heated enclosure or a pour delay is always less than a failed pour.

## 4. Wrong Water-to-Cement Ratio

Adding water to a stiff mix on site is tempting. It's also wrong. Every added litre of water reduces compressive strength and increases porosity — meaning your structure absorbs water and degrades faster.

## 5. Not Accounting for Formwork Pressure

Fresh concrete exerts significant lateral pressure on formwork. Underestimating this leads to blowouts, which are dangerous and expensive. Always calculate full hydrostatic pressure for deep pours.

The good news? All five of these are fixable with process, not money. Train your crews, enforce the checklist, and the savings follow.
